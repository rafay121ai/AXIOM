I grew up on a sandbar called Clearwater Beach. I didn't realize it at the time, but I had all kinds of **learning glitches** that now would be diagnosed as ADHD and dyslexia. School was tough; I couldn't really read and retain information, so I had to learn different ways of learning. But at an early age, I started working on my **mindset**. I became an absolute student of how to manage my own thoughts and particularly my negative self-thoughts. It started for me with **Wayne Dyer**. 

My dad was a trial attorney and strategic thinker, and my mom was an artist who was very shy and struggled with self-worth. I am a blend of both; I got the creativity and the ability to think a few steps ahead. One of my favorite Wayne Dyer quotes is: **"Nothing will matter more than your opinion of yourself. Do things that make you proud. Be kind. Back yourself up."**

**Chip Conley:** How did you get introduced to Wayne Dyer?

**Sara Blakely:** I was a sophomore at Clearwater High, and my parents were separating. When my dad was moving out, he handed me a cassette tape series in a plastic binder titled **"How to be a No-Limit Person."** He told me, "Sweetie, I wish I was your age when I discovered this instead of age 40." I was a 15- or 16-year-old in a lot of pain, so I was open to listening to them. I sat on my bedroom floor, started to cry, and thought, "I have spent 16 years in school being taught *what* to think, and **no one has ever taught me *how* to think.**" I realized I could be in the driver's seat of my thoughts. I started hearing about manifesting, the law of attraction, visualization, and not fearing failure. 

**Chip Conley:** You went to **Florida State (FSU)** and decided law school was not going to be for you?

**Sara Blakely:** Not once, but twice. I'm a terrible test taker, which was tragic because I was a champion debater. I knew I would have been an epic lawyer, but the **Universe was nudging me** and letting me know I was off-course. 

**Chip Conley:** The Universe is sitting on your shoulder a lot. How does it come to you?

**Sara Blakely:** Everyone has access to a **universal energy and all-knowingness**. It’s a channel, like an old-fashioned car radio where you turn the dial through the fuzz until the song comes in crystal clear. I started creating mechanisms as a little girl to feel more comfortable with existential thoughts. I created good luck symbols, like the number **27** or my good luck song, **"Sara Smile."** When I hear that song on the radio, it means I'm safe and on the right track. I’ve framed key decisions throughout my whole life around these signs.

**Chip Conley:** You started selling **fax machines door-to-door**. I bet you were pretty persuasive.

**Sara Blakely:** I carried them with me in **pantyhose and high heels** in the Florida summer. I’d walk in and ask, "Do you need a fax machine?" and usually get escorted out. I did that for **seven years**. I realized I was in the "wrong movie." I went home and wrote down what I was good at, and the only thing in the "good" column was sales. I asked myself *why* I was good at it, and realized I loved offering something that improves someone's life. I set the intention: **"Universe, give me the idea [to change millions of lives] and I will not squander it."** 

Two years later, I couldn't figure out what to wear under white pants because undergarments showed. I realized a revolutionary new undergarment was needed. I cut the feet out of my pantyhose because the material was thin like tissue paper and breathable. It created a perfect canvas without lines or bulges. I had **five grand**, no business classes, and no contacts, but I had the Universe. I looked up manufacturers in North Carolina and **wrote my own patent** using a book from Barnes & Noble. 

**Chip Conley:** Spanx was almost immediately successful. By 2012, *Forbes* called you the youngest self-made female billionaire. How did you scale it?

**Sara Blakely:** I focused on three things: **make it, sell it, build awareness**. I didn't know how to read a spreadsheet, but I knew women needed this. I hit the road for two years, selling it in stores like Neiman Marcus myself because I wasn't going to leave the fate of my idea to a sleepy corner of the hosiery department. I bought stands from Office Depot and put them at every cash register to get **"Baby out of the corner."** We didn't spend money on advertising for 16 years; it was all organic word-of-mouth. I led with the **problem**: that men had been making women's undergarments for a long time and they were uncomfortable. 

**Chip Conley:** At age 50, you decided to sell a majority share to **Blackstone**. Why?

**Sara Blakely:** I had been the "Spanx girl" for 21 years; my identity was meshed with the company. I started getting a little "tap" from spirit that I needed to move on. I wanted to **free up my mental share**. 80% of my mental share for 21 years was on Spanx. I wanted to see what my mind would think about if I didn't own it. It was very hard to let go; I mourned it. I was in a lot of grief, which is how I found you at **MEA**. 

**Chip Conley:** You took a **"midlife atrium"** to reimagine yourself. And then **Sneaks** started to sneak into your consciousness.

**Sara Blakely:** I moved from **"prove" energy to "improve" energy**. I had been in "prove" energy since I was three, wanting to elevate women and get the respect of men. I had a vision to invent a **comfortable high heel**. I asked the Universe for a sign and saw Gail King interview a shoe designer who said, "If someone tells me my shoes are comfortable, I take it as an insult." I decided to try, knowing nothing about shoes. 

I wanted a **3-inch stiletto that feels like a sneaker**. I redistributed the weight so it's not all on the ball of the foot and gave the whole foot support. Four years in, we almost pulled the plug because it wasn't comfortable enough. Then I had an epiphany: I cut a sneaker and a high heel in half, glued them together, and walked around my house for three days. That pivot led to the final design. 

**Chip Conley:** What has it been like to launch the "sequel" after such a huge first success?

**Sara Blakely:** I don't care about the second act or trying to outdo the first one. But I did **pause the launch for a whole year** because I woke up in a panic. I wondered if I was just getting back on the same treadmill or if I was doing this for the right reasons. I also realized I was **chemically imbalanced from perimenopause**. I had PMDD—perimenopause on steroids. Once I started taking progesterone, I felt like my old self again. 

**Chip Conley:** What’s next for you?

**Sara Blakely:** I want to be in the **present moment**. As a founder, I was always in a state of fight-or-flight. I’ll never retire; it’s not in my DNA. If I retired to a garden, I’d have a business selling tomatoes in two days. 

**Chip Conley:** What's your **wisdom bumper sticker**?

**Sara Blakely:** I have two. The first is: **"The more you experience in life, the more you have to offer others."** It gives me the courage to say yes to things that scare me. The second is: **"What you don't know can be your greatest asset if you let it."** It means you won't do it like everybody else.
